Designing viability gap funding for storage-hybrid auctions

Sep 2025 · Procurement

How to balance tariff caps with performance-based support while protecting system needs and investor appetite.

Balancing cost and reliability

Storage hybrids deliver flexibility but raise capital costs. Viability gap funding works when it rewards reliable dispatch instead of cheap megawatt-hours alone, nudging bidders toward designs that hold frequency and reduce curtailment.

Structuring the subsidy

Pair tariff caps with milestone-linked grants or contracts-for-differences that fade as performance improves. Define availability metrics for the storage block and translate them into claw-back logic so treasuries can defend the subsidy while investors keep clarity on upside.

Market-facing safeguards

Grid codes and dispatch protocols should clarify who can call on storage and how quickly it must respond. Aligning dispatch rights between the system operator and off-taker keeps hybrid plants from gaming imbalance charges while still meeting the renewable objectives of the programme.

Execution checklist

Build test events into the PPA, lock in telemetry formats before bid award, and stress-test developer models for split revenue streams. Simple annexes with state-of-charge reporting templates often avoid disputes later.
