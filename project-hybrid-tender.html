Project reference

Hybrid solar, wind, and storage competitive tender

Namibia · 2024 · Hybrid procurement

Bankable PPA and bid evaluation design paired with firmness modelling, frequency response obligations, and stress-tested cashflows tailored to Namibia’s grid conditions.

Narrative

We reworked the procurement documents so reliability counted as much as price. Hour-by-hour traces for solar, wind, and storage combinations fed into evaluation scoring, letting the off-taker see how different storage durations and dispatch rules would hold up under weak-grid conditions.

Tender rules included response obligations for primary and secondary frequency support. That clarity allowed bidders to price grid services explicitly rather than bury obligations inside energy tariffs, and it gave the system operator a clearer view of how hybrid plants would contribute when inertia is low.

Approach

The team aligned lenders, treasury, and the system operator around a PPA that reflected true operating limits. A bidder workshop walked developers through telemetry requirements, availability testing, and the dispatch hierarchy so that operational data, not negotiation, would decide performance claims after signing.

Key facts

  • Role Transaction advisor to the utility and ministry with embedded grid and legal support.
  • Scope Firmness modelling, PPA design, viability gap rules, and evaluation framework.
  • Outcome Tender ready with balanced risk allocation, clear frequency obligations, and investor buy-in.
  • Team Power systems, procurement, bankability specialists, and lender liaison support.
