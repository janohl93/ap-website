Project reference

Central corridor grid reinforcement

Private-participation roadmap and dispatch analysis aligning utility, investors, and regulator around staged reinforcement and future wheeling growth.

Narrative

Reinforcing the central corridor required blending public and private capital without overburdening tariffs. We traced flow constraints across dry- and wet-year dispatch runs, flagging where transformers and protection schemes would bottleneck new IPP injections. That evidence showed how staged upgrades could unlock regional trade while keeping tariffs predictable.

The roadmap sequenced investments so reliability improved ahead of demand growth, giving the regulator confidence to approve tariff adjustments linked to measurable service gains. Communities along the corridor now had a visible timeline for when outages would ease and when new load connections could be processed.

Approach

Investor roundtables tested different availability regimes and termination points. A shared data room with clear dispatch assumptions helped bidders and the utility debate risks with the same evidence base, while the system operator stress-tested evacuation risks under planned maintenance to fine-tune performance incentives.
